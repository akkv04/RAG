SECTION 1: LEAVE AND ATTENDANCE POLICY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 Annual Leave
Employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Leave
accrues at a rate of 1.67 days per month. Unused leave may be carried over to
the following year up to a maximum of 10 days. Any leave in excess of 10 days
will be forfeited at the end of the calendar year. Employees must submit leave
requests at least 5 business days in advance via the HR portal.

1.2 Sick Leave
Full-time employees receive 10 days of paid sick leave per year. Sick leave does
not carry over and resets on January 1st each year. A medical certificate is
required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days. Employees must notify their
direct manager before 9:00 AM on the first day of absence.

1.3 Parental Leave
Primary carers are entitled to 16 weeks of paid parental leave. Secondary carers
receive 4 weeks of paid leave. Leave must commence within 12 months of the
child's birth or adoption date. Employees must provide at least 8 weeks notice
before commencing parental leave.

1.4 Unpaid Leave
Employees may apply for unpaid leave for personal reasons up to a maximum of
12 weeks per year. Approval is at the discretion of the department head and HR.
Continuity of service is maintained during unpaid leave periods.

SECTION 2: REMOTE WORK POLICY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Eligibility
Remote work arrangements are available to permanent employees who have completed
their 6-month probationary period. Casual and contract employees are not eligible
for formal remote work agreements unless otherwise specified in their contract.

2.2 Approved Work-from-Home Days
Employees may work from home a maximum of 3 days per week. At least 2 days per
week must be spent in the office unless a full-remote exception has been approved
by the executive team. Teams must coordinate to ensure at least 50% of team
members are present on any given in-office day.

2.3 Equipment and Security
The company will provide a laptop and necessary peripherals for remote work.
Employees must use a company-approved VPN when accessing internal systems from
home. Use of personal devices for work is not permitted without explicit IT
approval. Employees are responsible for maintaining a secure and ergonomic
workspace at home.

2.4 Performance and Availability
Remote employees must be reachable via Teams during core hours of 10:00 AM to
3:00 PM in their local timezone. Response time to messages must not exceed
30 minutes during core hours. Managers may revoke remote work privileges if
performance or availability issues are identified.

SECTION 3: CODE OF CONDUCT AND ETHICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 Professional Behaviour
All employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, and partners with
respect and professionalism at all times. Harassment, bullying, or discrimination
of any kind will not be tolerated and may result in immediate termination.
Employees must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to their manager and
HR within 5 business days of becoming aware of the conflict.

3.2 Confidentiality
Employees must not disclose confidential company information, client data, or
intellectual property to external parties without written approval from the
Legal department. This obligation continues for 2 years after the end of
employment. Breaches of confidentiality may result in disciplinary action and
legal proceedings.

3.3 Gifts and Hospitality
Employees may accept gifts from clients or vendors up to a value of $50 AUD.
Gifts exceeding this value must be declared to HR within 48 hours and may be
refused or donated to charity. Offering gifts to clients or government officials
in excess of $100 AUD requires prior approval from the Chief Compliance Officer.

3.4 Social Media
Employees must not post content that identifies them as an ACME employee when
discussing competitors, clients, or company matters without prior approval from
the Communications team. Personal opinions expressed online must not be
attributed to ACME Corporation.

SECTION 4: IT AND DATA SECURITY POLICY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 Password Requirements
All system passwords must be at least 14 characters long and include a mix of
uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters. Passwords must be changed
every 90 days. Reuse of the last 10 passwords is not permitted. Multi-factor
authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all systems that support it.

4.2 Data Classification
Company data is classified into four tiers: Public, Internal, Confidential, and
Restricted. Restricted data includes personal employee records, financial
forecasts, and client contracts. Restricted data must be encrypted at rest and
in transit. Access to Restricted data is granted on a need-to-know basis only.

4.3 Acceptable Use
Company-issued devices must not be used for personal business activities,
cryptocurrency mining, or accessing illegal content. Personal use of company
devices is permitted in moderation provided it does not impact productivity.
All activity on company devices and networks is subject to monitoring.

4.4 Incident Reporting
Employees must report any suspected security breach, phishing attempt, or data
loss to the IT Security team within 1 hour of discovery. Failure to report a
known incident is a disciplinable offence. The IT Security team can be reached
at security@acme.com.au or via the internal helpdesk portal.

SECTION 5: EXPENSE AND REIMBURSEMENT POLICY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Travel Expenses
Domestic flights must be booked economy class unless the travel duration exceeds
4 hours, in which case premium economy is permitted. International flights over
10 hours may be booked in business class with VP-level approval. Hotel
accommodation must not exceed $250 AUD per night for domestic travel or $350 AUD
per night for international travel without prior approval.

5.2 Meal Allowances
Employees travelling for work may claim meal expenses up to $80 AUD per day for
domestic travel and $120 AUD per day for international travel. Alcohol may not
be claimed as a business expense unless part of an approved client entertainment
event. All meal claims require itemised receipts.

5.3 Submission Deadlines
Expense claims must be submitted within 30 days of the expense being incurred.
Claims submitted after 60 days will not be reimbursed regardless of
circumstances. All claims must be submitted via the Finance portal with original
receipts attached.

5.4 Approval Thresholds
Expenses under $500 AUD may be approved by a direct manager. Expenses between
$500 and $2,000 AUD require department head approval. Expenses exceeding $2,000
AUD require CFO approval. Emergency exceptions must be documented with a written
justification.

SECTION 6: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT POLICY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 Review Cycle
Performance reviews are conducted twice per year: mid-year in July and annual in
January. Employees and managers must complete self-assessments and manager
assessments respectively using the HR performance management platform.

6.2 Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)
Employees who receive a rating of "Below Expectations" in two consecutive reviews
may be placed on a formal Performance Improvement Plan. A PIP runs for 90 days
and includes specific, measurable goals agreed upon by the employee and manager.
Failure to meet PIP goals may result in termination of employment.

6.3 Promotions and Salary Reviews
Salary adjustments are considered annually in January. Promotions may be
recommended at any time but are subject to headcount approval from the relevant
VP. Employees must have a minimum of 12 months of tenure to be eligible for a
promotion.

SECTION 7: HEALTH, SAFETY AND WELLBEING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1 Workplace Safety
All employees must complete mandatory workplace health and safety training within
their first 30 days of employment. Employees must report any workplace hazards
or injuries to their manager and the People team immediately. The company
maintains a zero-tolerance policy for working under the influence of alcohol or
drugs.

7.2 Mental Health Support
ACME provides access to an Employee Assistance Program (EAP) offering up to
6 free confidential counselling sessions per year per employee. Employees
experiencing work-related stress or mental health challenges are encouraged to
speak with HR or their manager. All such discussions are treated with strict
confidentiality.

7.3 Ergonomics
Employees working from home or in the office may request an ergonomic assessment
through the People team. The company will subsidise up to $500 AUD toward
ergonomic equipment for home office setups, subject to manager approval.
